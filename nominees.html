Celebrating Excellence in Northern Uganda Cinema

2025 Award Nominees

The Northern Uganda Film Awards proudly presents the nominees for the 2025 ceremony. These talented filmmakers and performers represent the best of our region's vibrant cinema industry.

Best Feature Film

The Acholi King

Directed by Opio Brashan

A historical epic about the last independent ruler of Acholiland, blending traditional storytelling with stunning cinematography of Northern Uganda's landscapes.

Silent Tears

Directed by Grace Akello

A poignant drama exploring the lives of women in post-conflict Northern Uganda, praised for its authentic performances and sensitive direction.

Kony's Shadow

Directed by Otolo Sedrick

A gripping thriller about reconciliation and justice in communities affected by the LRA insurgency, notable for its balanced perspective.

Boda Boda Tales

Directed by Bodo Desderio

A vibrant comedy-drama following the interconnected lives of motorcycle taxi drivers in Gulu city, celebrated for its humor and local flavor.

Best Director

Opio Brashan

The Acholi King

For his visionary direction of this historical epic, seamlessly blending large-scale battle sequences with intimate character moments.

Grace Akello

Silent Tears

Her sensitive and nuanced direction drew extraordinary performances from a predominantly female cast in this powerful drama.

Otolo Sedrick

Kony's Shadow

Masterfully balanced tension and emotional depth in this complex story about post-conflict reconciliation.

Best Actor in a Leading Role

Okello Patrick

The Acholi King

Brought regal dignity and human vulnerability to his portrayal of the legendary Rwot (King) Awich.

Lakot Alfred

Kony's Shadow

His intense performance as a former child soldier struggling with reinvention was both raw and restrained.

Ocitti Robert

Boda Boda Tales

Displayed remarkable range in this comedic yet heartfelt performance as a boda boda driver with big dreams.

Best Actress in a Leading Role

Aciro Stella

Silent Tears

Her powerful yet subtle performance as a war widow rebuilding her life anchored the emotional core of the film.

Adong Judith

Queen of the Nile

Commanded the screen as a businesswoman navigating male-dominated spaces in 1980s Uganda.

Auma Fiona

The Acholi King

Brought strength and complexity to her role as the king's principal wife during turbulent times.

Best Documentary Film

Dancing Through the Pain

Directed by Acan Mary

Follows traditional dancers preserving cultural heritage while addressing trauma from the northern conflict.

The River Between Us

Directed by Kinyera Emmanuel

Explores the environmental and social impact of the Nile River on Northern Ugandan communities.

Rebuilding Hope

Directed by Lagot Sarah

A decade-long study of IDP camp residents returning home and rebuilding their lives with remarkable resilience.

Technical Achievement Awards

Best Cinematography

Ocen Daniel

The Acholi King

For breathtaking landscape shots and innovative battle sequence cinematography.

Lakara Simon

Silent Tears

Intimate, naturalistic lighting that enhanced the film's emotional realism.

Best Original Score

Okello Geoffrey

The Acholi King

Blending traditional Acholi instruments with orchestral arrangements.

Auma Prossy

Dancing Through the Pain

Innovative fusion of contemporary and traditional dance music.

Special Awards

Lifetime Achievement

Otim Tony

Pioneering filmmaker who established the first film production company in Northern Uganda in 1992.

Rising Star

Acan Brenda

Boda Boda Tales

At just 19, delivered a breakout comedic performance that stole every scene she was in.

Audience Choice

Boda Boda Tales

This crowd-pleasing comedy resonated strongly with audiences across the region.

The winners will be announced at the 2025 Northern Uganda Film Awards ceremony on December 14th at the Gulu Grand Hotel.
